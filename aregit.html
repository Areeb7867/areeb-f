Covid-19

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID‑19) is an infectious disease caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2).
It was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, Hubei, China, and has resulted in an ongoing pandemic. As of 12 August 2020,
more than 20.4 million cases have been reported across 188 countries and territories, resulting in more than 744,000 deaths. More than 12.6 million people
have recovered.


Precautions to prevent from Covid-19

1. Wash your hands frequently and carefully

Use warm water and soap and rub your hands for at least 20 seconds. Work the lather to your
wrists, between your fingers, and under your fingernails. You can also use an antibacterial and
antiviral soap.

2. Avoid touching your face

SARS-CoV-2 can live on some surfaces for up to 72 hours. You can get the virus on your hands if
you touch a surface like: gas pump handle, your cell phone, a doorknob.

3. Stop shaking hands and hugging people — for now

Similarly, avoid touching other people. Skin-to-skin contact can transmit SARS-CoV-2 from one
person to another.
